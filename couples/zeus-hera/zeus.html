Who is Zeus?

Zeus is the supreme god in Greek mythology and the ruler of Mount Olympus. He controls the sky, thunder, and lightning, and is often depicted holding a powerful lightning bolt. As king of the gods, Zeus maintains order among both gods and humans.

He is the youngest son of Cronus and Rhea. After defeating his father and the Titans, Zeus and his siblings divided the universe: Zeus took the sky, Poseidon the sea, and Hades the underworld.

  • The punishment of Prometheus
  • The story of Europa

Personality

Zeus is powerful, authoritative, and protective of divine law. However, he is also known for his temper and numerous romantic affairs, which often caused conflict among the gods, especially with Hera.

The sky trembles when Zeus speaks.
